Design Thinking Process

How I applied design thinking to create user-centered websites

1

Empathize

I interviewed my client Sara to understand her needs. She wanted to share traditional Trinidadian recipes and her family stories. Her favorite colors are red and gold. I learned that food connects her to her grandmother and culture.

2

Define

I created a problem statement: "Sara needs a way to share her traditional recipes and family stories so as to preserve her cultural heritage and connect with others who love Caribbean food."

3

Ideate

I brainstormed ideas for colors, fonts, layout, and content. I created an idea board with red and gold colors, recipe lists, images of Trinidadian dishes, and a personal story section about her grandmother.

4

Prototype

I built a basic HTML/CSS prototype with headings, lists, images, and a color scheme. I focused on making the layout clean and the content easy to read.

5

Test

I showed my prototype to classmates and my client. Based on feedback, I added more personal stories, a family photo, and better recipe descriptions. Testing helped me improve the website significantly.

How Design Thinking Improved My Website

Design thinking taught me to focus on the user first. Instead of just coding what I thought looked good, I listened to what Sara actually needed. This made my final product more meaningful and effective. The process of empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test gave me a clear roadmap to follow.
